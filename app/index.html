The Live Wires workflow in five steps

Step 1. Write your content

Writers, export your content in HTML. Then designers, paste that lovely goodness into your naked template.

Tip: Think ahead

Stick with the format your final product has adopted. For example, if your CMS uses Markdown, write the content in Markdown, then export it into HTML so you can build your fancy prototype around the content without having to reflow and reformat.

Step 2. Apply the styles & section up your content

Include the Live Wires styles. Then divide your content into sections using the included structure classes for an instant mobile wireframe.

Tip: Focus on flow

Apply the .content class anywhere you want to hide paragraphs and lists text so you can present interaction without the distraction. This way youâ€™re working content first, but can focus on organizational hierarchy when showing pages to your team or clients.

Step 3. Stretch the focus

Now that your linear content flow has been organized, stretch that browser and start arranging your content horizontally and vertically using our handy grid system and your own layout classes.

Tip: Stay lo-fi

Work quickly, loosely, and in the lowest fidelity possible. Your wireframes should focus on interaction and flow over layout.

Step 4. Style the guide

Our Live Wires style guide becomes your project’s style guide. Go through each page:

  • Set the type
  • Style your elements
  • Build your components
  • Combine to make modules
  • Add your own modules and components
  • Tear out what you don’t need
  • Add new sections

Make this guide your own.

As you style the guide, you’re simultaneously adding a rough sheen to you wireframes.

Tip: Typography first

Live Wires glows with joy when you begin with type.

Step 5. Refine your prototype

Tighten up each of your templates to get them audience ready. Add additional components and modules to create a flexible design you can update any time and copy them back into your style guide for good measure.

Before you know it, you’ll have a stylish working prototype, ready to integrate with the CMS or app of your choice.

Tip: Switch roles

Use Page Layers to quickly convert your templates & style guide to PSDs for “sketching” ideas. Move back and forth between generating ideas in an image editor & refining in code.
